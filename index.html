The Problem
Legal billing apportionment is slow, manual, and error-prone.

When a single case spans multiple insurers and policy periods, allocating legal fees is a nightmare. Coverage counsel spends hours manually parsing invoices and calculating each carrier's share — often getting it wrong. There was no software purpose-built for this workflow.

The Solution
AI parsing + day-exact math = insurer-ready output in minutes.

LexAlloc ingests legal invoices via AI, extracts every line item, and runs apportionment automatically using one of three allocation methods. The result: a verified split by carrier, downloadable as an insurer-ready demand letter. A 3% platform fee on processed apportionments creates a scalable revenue model.

Allocation Methods
Three approaches. One platform.
  • Pro-Rata TOR — allocates by days each policy was on-risk during the service period (day-exact)
  • Equal Shares — splits costs evenly across all active carriers for each party
  • Limits-Proportional — weights each carrier's share by their policy limit
  • AI Method Advisor recommends the right approach based on invoice characteristics
Platform Features
Built for coverage counsel
  • AI invoice parsing — extract all line items from uploaded PDFs automatically
  • Multi-org admin panel with platform-wide revenue tracking and user management
  • Side-by-side method comparison before committing to an apportionment
  • Insurer-ready demand letter generation after apportionment is confirmed
My Role
End-to-end product ownership

I owned the entire product — concept, business model, UX/UI design, system architecture, AI parsing pipeline, apportionment logic, admin panel, and go-to-market framing. Built using AI-assisted development. No external engineering team.

Tech Stack & Business Model
SaaS with a transaction-based revenue layer
React Supabase (Postgres + Auth) AI Invoice Parsing Netlify Multi-Org Admin PDF Generation

3% fee on all processed apportionments · Target market: coverage counsel firms · Industry size: $350B/yr legal billing

Challenges
The hard parts
  • Designing the Pro-Rata TOR calculation to be truly day-exact across overlapping policy periods — billing service dates don't always align cleanly with policy effective dates, requiring edge-case handling at the line-item level.
  • Building an AI parsing pipeline that reliably extracts structured data from heterogeneous legal invoice formats — different law firms format billing records differently, with inconsistent date fields, descriptions, and rate structures.
Outcome

A production-deployed LegalTech SaaS platform that automates one of the most tedious and high-stakes workflows in insurance coverage law. LexAlloc targets a $350B/yr industry with a simple, defensible monetization model — take 3% of every apportionment processed — and gives coverage counsel a tool that didn't exist before.
